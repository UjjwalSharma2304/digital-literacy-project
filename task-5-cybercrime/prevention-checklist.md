## **Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist**



1. UPI PIN Rule: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. A PIN is only required for authorizing payments or checking your balance.

2\. Verify QR Codes: Be sceptical of QR codes sent over WhatsApp or SMS by unknown "buyers." Scanning a malicious code can trigger a "Collect Request" on your account.

3\. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Enable 2FA on all academic and personal accounts, especially your Gmail and LinkedIn, to add a second layer of security beyond a password.

4\. Use Official App Stores: Only download banking or social media apps from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store to avoid "sideloading" malware or keyloggers.

5\. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Transactions: Never log into your bank account or make UPI payments while connected to open college or public Wi-Fi networks.

6\. Secure Your social media: Set your personal profiles (Instagram/Facebook) to private and audit your followers list to prevent identity harvesters from gathering personal data.

7\. Identify Phishing: Check the sender’s email address carefully for typos or "spoofed" domains (e.g., google-support.co instead of google.com) before clicking links.

8\. Report Immediately: If you are a victim of fraud, report it within the "Golden Hour" to improve your chances of recovering funds
